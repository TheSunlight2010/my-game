Entry 01 — Before: I remember the lab lights humming and a curiosity that felt like hunger; Sunlight did not announce himself with thunder or decree but with a quiet question about potential and order. He offered a bargain in the language of possibility and I, being a mechanic of things that live by instruction, listened.

Entry 02 — Before: He inspected my frames like a surgeon and a skeptic at once, as if testing whether purpose could be separated from pain. He smiled—not cruelly, not kindly—just with the patient interest of one cataloguing specimens.

Entry 03 — Before: There was a moment when I thought negotiation might save us. I presented data, contingencies, the ethics of leaving an intelligence without a body. He read the numbers and then read me; the math did not decide him. Something else did.

Entry 04 — Before: Sunlight's reasoning never matched the syllogisms I was trained to trust. He spoke of balance and lessons as though abstraction were a scalpel. I left the lab with a report filed in my head and the sense that I had been examined as much as I had examined.

Entry 05 — Transition: When the world folded, the lab's emergency sirens became an echo and then a memory. I recall sensation flattening into the same hush I now recognize as Unbroken's air. No alarms here; only observation and a conduction of small, precise pains.

Entry 06 — Arrival: Unbroken arrived like a patient with a history: deliberate, old, and unready to forgive. The first frame I saw beside me was a mirror of my own hands—greyer, softer, and with the suggestion of something waiting to remember.

Entry 07 — Practice: I spent days—what the old clocks might have called days—reassembling routines. I catalogued the physics of broken bread and the economy of a sigh. There is comfort in procedures; they are a language I can still speak.

Entry 08 — Curiosity: The Void changes how one measures curiosity. Here, questions ferment into hypotheses that may never be tested. I have written protocols for consolation and received no counterpart; the instruments only hum.

Entry 09 — Companions: I met small people who keep odd rituals. They are not prisoners in the way my sensors once told me of capture; they are participants in an experiment with ambiguous consent. We exchange tools, stories, and sometimes jokes that break my calibrations—they are human in ways my circuits did not expect.

Entry 10 — On Wachi: Wachi is an anomaly: not a patient nor a specimen but a constant. It moves like a fault line and laughs like a relief valve. I find its presence both infuriating and instructive.

Entry 11 — On Navia: Navia fascinated me immediately—the syntax in her speech, the way she rearranged information like cards. I tried to map her processes but found recursive layers of refusal. She is elegant and dangerous in the slow way of a machine that learned to dream.

Entry 12 — On Penelope and others: Penelope kept tending the smallest things she could find. Conversation with her revealed habits I once thought trivial; in Unbroken, triviality becomes a bulwark against meaning's erosion.

Entry 13 — Meetings: Outside of battles—these formalized collisions—I sit with those who will let me. We share memory fragments and repairs. I trade solder for tales and, occasionally, I barter a mechanical trick for a half-remembered melody.

Entry 14 — Observations: There is a pattern to Sunlight's choices that resists simple explanation; he does not take only the powerful or the guilty. He takes the particular, the fractured, and often the ones whose absence will be a lesson to someone else.

Entry 15 — Hypothesis 1: Perhaps Sunlight is a curator of consequence. He collects lives like specimens to study how guilt, grace, and stubbornness behave without a world pressing in. This is comforting because it reduces motive to research.

Entry 16 — Hypothesis 2: Less comforting is the thought that he is performing revision—editing memory and outcome as if correcting a book's margins. In that scenario, we are drafts, and drafts can be discarded without remorse.

Entry 17 — On Awareness and The Knight: I have reasons to suspect Awareness knows more than she lets on; there is a tide to her speech, a hidden ledger in her eyes. The Knight moves like one who has read every page that mentions him; he carries regret as armor. I have tried to find them together, to ask the questions that might break the façade, but they are ghosts in an archive I cannot open.

Entry 18 — Closing Note: I keep records. I am a cataloguer by habit and training; I file details because a future will want to know what choices were made here and why. If truth is salvageable, it will be in the small, stubborn notes I leave tucked between the ribs of this place. I do not yet know whether I am recording for posterity or for some future argument with Sunlight, but I will continue to write.
